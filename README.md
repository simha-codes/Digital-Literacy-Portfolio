# CSE0001: Digital Literacy Project Portfolio 

**Hey there! 👋** My name is Vishwasimha, and I'm a first-year B.Tech student CSE(AI/ML) at VIT Bhopal. 

This repository is my final submission for the CSE0001 Digital Literacy course. I built this portfolio to document what I've learned about online safety, professional communication, and setting up my digital footprint as an engineering student.

Here is a breakdown of everything included in this repo:

---

### 📂 Folder Structure & Tasks

#### 1. `task-1-presentation/`
* **What's inside:** A Canva infographic I designed to explain the core pillars of digital literacy. 
* **Why I made it:** To create a quick, easy-to-read guide for my batchmates about being smart and safe online.

#### 2. `task-2-portfolio/`
* **What's inside:** Screenshots of my newly set up GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle profiles.
* **Why I made it:** Just getting my professional digital footprint started early so I'm ready for hackathons, open-source projects, and internships later in my degree.

#### 3. `task-3-platforms/`
* **What's inside:** Evidence of a beginner coding challenge I solved on HackerRank, plus screenshots of a Google Form/Sheet I made.
* **Live Link:** [My Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSeqaorNSefNXPc1tcmPdkVAbaEctgvWHEVGqJBKaFfstvkPlA/viewform?usp=sf_link)
* **Why I made it:** To practice basic problem-solving logic and figure out how to collect and manage data using cloud collaboration tools.

#### 4. `task-4-communication/`
* **What's inside:** Two drafted professional emails (one to a professor asking for help, one to a recruiter) and a Social Media Privacy Checklist.
* **Why I made it:** Because sending a proper email is a lot different than texting, and I needed to make sure my personal social media accounts are actually private before applying for jobs.

#### 5. `task-5-cybercrime/`
* **What's inside:** A short case study on the UPI "Collect Request" scam and a practical prevention checklist for college students.
* **Why I made it:** To break down exactly how scammers are tricking students on second-hand marketplaces and list a few simple rules (like never entering your PIN to receive money) to avoid getting hacked.
